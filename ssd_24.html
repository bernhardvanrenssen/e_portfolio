Contributions & Evaluation of our group (Team 1) projects

Unit 6 – Design Document

--LINK TO DESIGN DOCUMENT--

In this module we were asked to develop an application with a secure repository as a team, and in the assignment of Unit 6, we would need to submit our design document with all the specifications.

It took a couple of weeks for us to start this project. Our first team meeting was when we had to compile our group’s thoughts on a secure programming language prior to unit 3 of this module. We then had a team vote on the topic and made a list of specific skills available within our group. Interaction was initially very sparse but increased as the project progressed.

We had weekly team meetings and strict deliverables which were set in every meeting to ensure that we met our goal on completing the design document in time.

--MINUTES OF TEAM MEETINGS--

My contributions to the Design Documents

As we had different strengths within our group, I knew my technical skills were maybe not the best in our group, but in return offered to take lead on this Design Document. My further contributions were:

  • Organize team meetings weekly
  • Wrote-up the initial framework for the document
  • Ensured that team members delivered their individual parts on-time and within word limits
  • Revised each contribution to fit into the overall story
  • Ensured that the design document reflected what was required in the brief
  • Responsible for writing the Introduction and Problem Statement
  • Responsible for writing the Scope of design and project assumptions
  • Editing of the document
  • Formatting and submission of the document.

Unit 11 – Final Coding Output

--LINK TO PROJECT--

As per the brief, we needed to build and deliver a working demo of the work outlined in our Unit 6 design document. This was a much more practical and intensive project, but we had established that we had a very broad range of skills within our team members.

Once we decided on the high-level architecture and components that needed to be built, we soon realized that we needed more regular team meetings. After completing the first part of this project (design document), the team really started to work well together, and team meetings were much more productive and flowing better. We had some issues due to different time zones but found a time in the week that fitted most of us, and never had issues with members not showing up.

With the different expertise in the group, we divided our team members into ‘microservice team leaders’ – essentially each member had the lead on a different microservice used (whether AWS database, Google Cloud APIs, or our Python modules managed in Github).

My contributions to the Coding Output

Having the least amount of practical experience in developing within this team, I offered run the team meetings, but not take lead in any technical aspect. One of our team members developed a simple flow-diagram, and we assigned each phase to each one of our members. My contributions were:

  • Run and coordinate team meetings
  • Team liaison person with the module coordinator for any questions
  • Responsible for the development of the CRUD data operations in Python
  • Black-box testing
  • White-box testing
  • Bug fixing
  • Ensuring that all code related and followed our initial Design Document as closely as possible
