Mid-Module Assignment: System Design

DESIGN OF A SYSTEM TO MANAGE SELF-SERVICE CHECKOUT AT A WELL-KNOWN STREET SUPERMARKET

I started out by designing the classes that were put forward as the customer's requirement. This was then followed by a figure on the relationships between the classes, to map out how the information would flow through the system:


The class and relationships are summarized below:

A. Composition - Each customer has only 1 Loyalty Schema, which will be destroyed when the instance of the Customer class is destroyed
B. Association - The LoyaltySchema class will determine the amount and operations on calculating the amount of LoyaltyPoints.
C. Aggregation - ShoppingBasket exists of 1 to many Products, which will still exist if the ShoppingBasket is destroyed.
D. Inheritance - Each PaymentMethod class (Apple Pay, Android Pay, Cash, Card etc.) inherits from the parent Payment class, but each child class also has a unique method to authorize payments.
E. Aggregation - A transaction consists of 1 or more Payment instances.
F. Aggregation - a Transaction also requires 1 ShoppingBasket
G. Association – a Staff instance can manually perform certain operations on a Transaction
H. Association – Staff will be alerted on any issue raised by StockControl, and will be able to perform certain operations on this class
I. Association – After a Transaction is completed, points will be assigned to a customer’s LoyaltySchema
J. Aggregation – StockControl manages operations on all Products.
